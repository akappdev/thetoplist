10Breaking The Bank Of England

On September 16, 1992, Britain’s pound crashed out the European Exchange Rate Mechanism (ERM) with a thud that was heard around the world. Around £3.3 billion was wiped from the government’s coffers, exacerbating an already bleak recession. Britain was humiliated, the pound was weakened, and people’s livelihoods destroyed. The man behind it all? George Soros.

At the time, Soros was working as a currency speculator with the Quantum Fund. He led the charge of speculators who attacked the pound, buying up gilts, selling them like crazy, then buying them back again at cheaper prices. While this was legal, it was also immoral. Soros later said he’d made £1 billion that day selling sterling he didn’t own.

The effects for the pound were so brutal that the day became known as Black Wednesday. Even now, almost 25 years later, Soros is still notorious for his attack on the currency.
